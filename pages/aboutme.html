Hey, I'm Kade

I am an incoming Freshman at Utah State University, studying Mechanical Engineering. I am interested in most branches of engineering, but Mechanical and Computer Engineering in particular fascinate me. I have been making engineering projects since middle school, when I took my first robotics and engineering design classes.

You can find a summary of those projects here.

Although I really like both Mechanical and Computer Engineering, when it came time for me to decide on my major, I went with Mechanical. This is because even though I really appreciate the work that accompanies computers, CAD software is my forte. As of writing this web page, I have experience with Autodesk Inventor, Fusion360, Onshape, and Blender. During my first semester at USU, I will also be learning Solidworks. Of the 5, I have taught classes for over 3 months on OnShape, and a User certification with Inventor. To accompany these skills, I have a heavy amount of experience working with 3D-printing. I bought my first 3D printer at the end of 8th grade, a Monoprice Mini Delta. Since then, I have printed countless knick-knacks that have been helpful to me in my daily life. Of course, that also meant learning how the printer worked in order to troubleshoot errors I encountered with it. More recently, I upgraded to a Creality CR-10 Mini. While both of these printers have 'Mini' in their name, the CR-10 Mini is far from the minuscule size of the Mini Delta. This printer has allowed me to be much, much more ambitious with my projects, removing my previous size constraint.
Beyond engineering, I have interest and experience in graphic design, rock climbing, and swimming. The summer before my senior year of high school, I taught a class at my local community college on how to use Adobe Illustrator. That experience taught me a lot about how to use Adobe's Creative Cloud. I have since used that knowledge to perform work for a 3D texturing graphics job and, more heavily, this website.
