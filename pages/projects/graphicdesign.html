Graphic Design

I created this design for a T-Shirt contest for my church's youth conference. The theme was 'beach', and our design constraints specified that we had to include the words 'A Great Work' somewhere in the design.

I had several ideas for what a beach-themed T-Shirt could look like, but the one I settled on was a palm tree. It was easily recognizable beach imagery, which was more than good enough for the contest. I used the trunk of the tree to contain the 'A Great Work' verbage, which was a good way for me to combine two of my constraints. In order to do that, I had to find a font that I liked. I ended up settling on Bungee. From there, I curved the text using the Arc Warp effect, since writing the text along an arc did not give me the effect I wanted. I cut slices out of the text to make it feel more like a tree using the knife tool. After making the leaves and coconuts, I felt like the design was a bit imbalanced. I experimented with putting a shovel to the right of the tree, but I ended up settling on a beach ball.

This is my website, as you are clearly aware. I built it the summer of 2021, writing all of the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It was difficult at first, but I eventually got the hang of the languages and how they interact well enough to make a decent-looking website. This project actually started all the way back in my freshman year of high school, when I was directed to make a portfolio website for my engineering classes. We were told to use Google Sites, but I was never satisfied with that. It felt too easy, and I didn't have nearly as much control over the way that it looked as I wanted. This solution is everything that I wanted and more, so I hope that you, reading this, like it.
